Issued by the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratization –United Arab EmiratesRemote Work Guidelines for the Private Sector 2 Remote Work is a flexible work arrangement that enables an employee to perform their professional duties, in whole or in part, outside the employer ’s premises. This is facilitated through the utilization of modern telecommunication technologies and is governed by the specific terms and conditions stipulated in the employment contract executed between the employee and the employer. Remote employees shall enjoy the same rights and be subject to the same obligations as their on -site counterparts. This parity extends to remuneration, working hours, leave entitlements, the provision of a safe working environment, and protection against all forms of discrimination, as set forth in the governing employment contract. What is the Definition of Remote Work? 3 Employer Obligations 4 Execute a clear, certified employment contract that expressly defines the employee ’s salary, the nature of the work, working hours, and the term of the agreement. Adhere to statutory working hours, ensuring the provision of mandated rest periods, weekly rest days, and the regulation of overtime in accordance with the applicable controls. Refrain from charging the employee any fees associated with employment or recruitment.Enable employees to exercise their rights to annual leave, sick leave, and all other forms of leave prescribed by law. In the case of a National employee, and in addition to the requirements set forth above, the employer shall be obligated to: •Register the National employee and remit monthly contributions to the Pensions and Social Insurance system, pursuant to the governing legislation, within one ( 1) month from the date of issuance of the work permit. •Report any amendments to the employment contract that may impact the terms of benefits under the "Nafis" program.Employer Obligations Provide the necessary technical services, electronic devices, and tools essential for the employee to perform their duties efficiently. Define specific tasks and performance metrics; it is recommended that all communication be conducted electronically and be time -stamped. Establish a mechanism for monitoring remote employees and produce all supporting documentation and records upon the request of the competent authority (Labor Inspector). Observe all legal procedures regarding the termination of employment contracts and ensure the disbursement of all end-of-service entitlements within the statutory deadlines. 5 The employee shall maintain the utmost confidentiality regarding all information and data accessed by virtue of their employment. The employee must refrain from disclosing trade secrets and return all company custody items to the employer upon the termination of their services. All tools and equipment provided for the work must be used appropriately. Misuse of such tools and equipment is strictly prohibited.The employee is required to work during the approved business days and the specific working hours stipulated in the employment contract. The employee must communicate and respond effectively to ensure the efficient completion of assigned tasks.The employee shall perform their assigned tasks personally under the direction and supervision of the employer or their authorized representative. The employee is strictly prohibited from subcontracting or outsourcing the work to any other employee or third party. The employee shall remain responsive via the approved communication channels during working hours and participate in virtual meetings as required by the exigencies of the work.The employee must adhere to the approved electronic monitoring and communication protocols, submitting productivity reports and deliverables in accordance with the agreed -upon schedules. Obligations of the Employee under the Remote Work Model In the case of a National employee, and in addition to the requirements set forth above, the employer shall be obligated to: •Promptly notify the Ministry of any change in their employment status or personal data that may affect their eligibility for benefits under the "Nafis" program. •Strictly adhere to the terms and standards governing the utilization of "Nafis" program benefits. 6 Under the remote work model, working hours are explicitly stipulated in the employment contract, in alignment with the other work models adopted by the establishment. The employee shall be granted a minimum of one ( 1) day of paid weekly rest, as determined by the employment contract or the establishment ’s internal bylaws. Any work performed in excess of the stipulated hours shall be deemed overtime. The employee shall be entitled to overtime pay calculated at the prescribed statutory rates.Working hours are set at 8 hours per day, with a maximum of 48 hours per week, unless the employment contract provides otherwise in accordance with the approved controls. The employee shall be entitled to one or more daily rest periods ; however, these intervals shall not be counted as part of the actual effective working hours. Working Hours for Remote Employees 7 Under the remote work model, employees shall be entitled to all statutory leaves prescribed by law. These entitlements shall be governed by the provisions of the employment contract, ensuring parity with all other employees. Remote employees shall be entitled to an annual leave of 30 days for each full year of service. For service exceeding six ( 6) months but less than one ( 1) year, the employee shall be entitled to two (2) days for each month of service.Upon the termination of the contractual relationship, the employee shall be entitled to a cash payment in lieu of their unused annual leave balance, calculated in accordance with the governing law of the United Arab Emirates. Health Insurance 11 The employer shall issue a Workers ’ Protection Insurance policy for employees operating under the remote work model adopted in the state, consistent with the requirements for all other employees within the establishment. This insurance program provides coverage for the employee ’s full financial entitlements, including unpaid wages as stipulated in the insurance policy, in the event the employer defaults on or refrains from payment. The policy provides coverage up to a maximu m limit of AED 20,000 for a duration of 30 months. How may an employee avail of the Workers ’ Protection Insurance? •An employee may submit a claim for the disbursement of their entitlements should the employer encounter financial distress or fail to settle wages and financial dues. •Claims shall be initiated following the filing of a labor complaint through the Ministry ’s official channels: The Ministry ’s Web Portal, The MOHRE Smart Application or The Toll-Free Call Center: 80084 . •The Ministry shall evaluate the claim in coordination with the Insurance Pool. Insurance premiums are determined based on the employee ’s basic wage and are categorized into two tiers: Tier 1: A premium not exceeding AED 5per month for those with a basic wage of AED 16,000 or less.
